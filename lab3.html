Introduction

Corgis have been around for age, and Queen Elizabeth has helped popularize them throughout Britain and the world as a whole.

About the Queen

The queen has owned dogs for generations. She's been breeding corgis for a long time and her final corgis are holly and willow.

History

Queen Elizabeth inherited the tradition of having corgis from the Queen Mother and King George The VI.

Throughout her life Elizabeth has owned over 30 Corgis and would always have at least two accompany her.

Holly and Willow are said to be the last corgis that the Queen will be breeding and they are movie stars! Yes you heard that right, they appeared in the opening ceremony for the Olympics in London alongside James Bond and the Queen.
